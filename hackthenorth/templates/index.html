Measure Your Text's Gender Bias

A 2011 study published in the American Psychological Association indicated that the use of gender-biased keywords in job descriptions directly correlates with the number of women who apply. According to the study, women are far less likely to demonstrate interest in positions whose descriptions include “male-gendered” words like “decisive,” “challenging,” and “dominate.” When a description includes words like “trust," “connect,” or “responsible,” women are more likely to “see themselves” in the position and apply.
